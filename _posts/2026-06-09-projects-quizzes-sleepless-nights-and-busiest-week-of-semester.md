### A Week Filled with Deadlines, Quizzes, and Continuous Learning

After the Eid-ul-Adha holidays discussed in my previous article, university life quickly returned to its fast pace. However, this particular week was very different from the usual routine. Almost every subject had some kind of evaluation, project, assignment, quiz, presentation, or practical activity waiting for us. It felt as if all deadlines had arrived at the same time.

From the very beginning of the week, students were busy preparing for multiple academic responsibilities. Managing one project is normally difficult, but during this period we were simultaneously handling projects from Object-Oriented Programming, Database Systems, and Digital Logic Design while also preparing for quizzes and assignments. As a result, most of our days were spent either in laboratories, classrooms, hostel rooms, or discussion sessions with friends.

One important lesson I learned during this period was that university success is not only about understanding concepts. It is also about managing time effectively when several important tasks overlap. There were days when we attended classes throughout the day and continued working on projects late into the night. Sometimes we would sleep only a few hours before waking up again to continue our work.

At the same time, both the Digital Logic Design theory quiz and Digital Logic Design lab quiz were approaching. Since these assessments covered multiple concepts that we had studied throughout the semester, proper preparation was necessary. I spent considerable time revising previous topics and reviewing practical examples to ensure that I understood both the theoretical and implementation aspects.

Fortunately, the preparation paid off. Alhamdulillah, I performed well in both quizzes. These assessments helped me realize how much my understanding of digital systems had improved since the beginning of the semester. Concepts that initially seemed complicated had gradually become easier through continuous practice and revision.

Another academic responsibility came from our Communication Skills course. Madam Sarah had assigned every student a teaching presentation. Instead of giving a traditional presentation, each student had to act as a teacher and explain a topic to the class while interacting with fellow students.

For my presentation, I selected Binary Conversion because it is one of the most fundamental concepts in computer science and computer engineering. I prepared slides explaining the differences between decimal and binary number systems, why computers rely on binary representation, and the methods used to convert decimal numbers into binary and vice versa.

Preparing the presentation helped me revise important concepts and improve my confidence in explaining technical topics. However, due to scheduling issues, Madam Sarah was unable to conduct the presentations during that week. Therefore, the activity was postponed until after the final examinations. Although the presentation was delayed, the preparation itself proved valuable and strengthened my understanding of the topic.

### Tiny Archer – My First Unity Game Development Project

One of the biggest highlights of this week was our Object-Oriented Programming project. Our teacher gave students the flexibility to choose between different development approaches. We could either use Visual Studio or explore Unity for game development. He encouraged students to try Unity because it could provide valuable practical experience that would be useful in future software and game development projects.

I decided to challenge myself by selecting Unity.

Initially, this decision created several difficulties because my laptop was not particularly powerful. Installing Unity required a significant amount of storage space and system resources. During the installation process, I encountered multiple issues related to storage limitations and performance. Several times I considered switching to an easier option, but I wanted to gain practical experience with a professional game development environment.

To solve the problem, I spent time cleaning my laptop, deleting unnecessary files, and creating enough space for the installation. After several attempts, Unity was finally installed successfully.

The next phase involved learning the software itself. Since it was my first serious experience with game development, almost everything was new to me. I spent many hours watching tutorials, experimenting with different tools, and learning how game objects, scenes, physics, and scripting worked together.

After approximately one to two weeks of continuous effort, I successfully developed a game called Tiny Archer. The game was designed as an open-world style environment where the player controls an archer character and interacts with the surroundings. Due to project limitations, I focused on building a functional section of the game rather than creating a complete large-scale world.

The development process was not easy. Many times features did not work as expected, and I had to spend hours debugging problems and testing solutions. However, every challenge taught me something new about programming, game logic, and software development.

When the project presentation day arrived, I demonstrated the game and explained its development process. Alhamdulillah, all the effort produced an excellent result. I received 100 out of 100 marks for the project. This achievement was especially meaningful because it reflected weeks of learning, experimentation, and persistence.

On the same day, we also had our OOP Lab Viva. Since I had spent considerable time understanding every aspect of the project, I was able to answer the questions confidently and perform well during the evaluation.

### Database Systems Project – Combining SQL, Machine Learning, and Real-World Problem Solving

Among all the activities of this busy week, the most ambitious and technically advanced work was undoubtedly our Database Systems Project. Unlike many classroom assignments that focus on a single concept, this project required us to combine multiple technologies and apply them to a real-world problem. It brought together database design, SQL implementation, Machine Learning concepts, data preprocessing, system integration, and user interface development.

Our project was based on predicting the risk of Coronary Heart Disease (CHD) using patient medical information. The idea behind the project was simple but meaningful: if doctors and patients can identify potential health risks early, preventive actions can be taken before serious health conditions develop. This made the project both technically challenging and socially relevant.

Under the supervision of Dr. Bilal Ahmad, we selected the Framingham Heart Study Dataset, a well-known medical dataset containing information collected from thousands of patients over many years. The dataset included attributes such as age, blood pressure, cholesterol levels, glucose measurements, smoking history, heart rate, and several other medical indicators that influence cardiovascular health.

The project was divided into three major components. The first component was the database system, where all patient information had to be stored in a structured manner. The second component was the Machine Learning model, which would analyze patterns within the dataset and learn how different medical factors contribute to heart disease risk. The third component was the user interface, which allowed users to enter patient information and receive predictions generated by the trained model.

My primary responsibility involved database-related tasks. I worked extensively on database design, normalization, table structures, SQL implementation, and ensuring that the data could be organized efficiently. Since the dataset contained numerous attributes and thousands of records, proper database design was essential. I applied normalization concepts and created relationships between tables to improve efficiency and reduce redundancy.

At the same time, we also worked on integrating the database with the Machine Learning pipeline. The overall workflow of our system was designed as:

Patient Data → Database → Machine Learning Model → Risk Prediction → Doctor/Patient

This workflow allowed information entered by users to move through the system in a logical and structured manner before producing a prediction result.

One aspect that I particularly appreciated during this project was the guidance provided by Dr. Bilal Ahmad. Throughout the semester, he consistently encouraged students to work on practical and meaningful projects rather than focusing only on theoretical exercises. His approach helped us understand how technologies such as databases, Machine Learning, and Artificial Intelligence can be combined to solve real-world problems.

Professor Bilal Ahmad has always emphasized the importance of using high-quality datasets in machine learning work. He often highlighted medical datasets specifically because they are collected with high precision and accuracy, making them more reliable for building predictive models. The Framingham Heart Study dataset was a perfect example of exactly the kind of data-driven, real-world problem he encouraged us to explore. Following his advice on his [Facebook page](https://www.facebook.com/Dr.BilalAhm) helped me stay connected with his latest thoughts on research and practical computing applications.

As development progressed, our project evolved from a simple database assignment into a complete prediction system. We designed an HTML-based interface where users could enter medical information. The entered data would then pass through the inference pipeline, interact with the Machine Learning model, and generate a prediction indicating whether the patient was likely to be at risk of Coronary Heart Disease within the next ten years.

Another important milestone during this period was the preparation of our project exhibition materials. Dr. Bilal instructed all groups to prepare a professional poster explaining their work. The poster needed to clearly communicate the problem, methodology, database design, Machine Learning component, workflow, and expected outcomes.

Creating the poster required additional effort because technical information had to be presented in a concise and visually appealing manner. Along with the poster, we were also asked to prepare a detailed project presentation video explaining the complete system and its functionality. In addition, a short promotional video of approximately 250 words was prepared to provide a quick overview of the project.

Preparing these materials consumed many hours because every component needed to accurately represent our work. We repeatedly reviewed diagrams, screenshots, explanations, and project flowcharts to ensure everything was presented professionally.

Although the workload was demanding, the project became one of the most rewarding experiences of the semester. It allowed us to apply SQL knowledge, database concepts, Machine Learning fundamentals, and software development practices within a single integrated system. More importantly, it showed us how technologies learned in the classroom can be transformed into solutions capable of addressing real-world problems.

Looking back, this project significantly strengthened my understanding of databases, improved my technical confidence, and introduced me to the practical side of Machine Learning integration. It also demonstrated the importance of teamwork, planning, documentation, and continuous learning when working on complex engineering projects.
