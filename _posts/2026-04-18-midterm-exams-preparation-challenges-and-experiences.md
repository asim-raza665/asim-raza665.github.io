### Midterm Exams and Preparation Journey

As my second semester continued, one of the most important phases of my academic journey was the midterm examinations. These exams were not only a test of my knowledge but also a test of my preparation, time management, and ability to handle pressure. Midterms play an important role in evaluating academic progress and helping students identify areas where improvement is needed before final examinations.

The midterm season was both exciting and challenging. Every subject required a different preparation strategy, and balancing multiple courses at the same time demanded discipline and consistency. During this period, I learned that success in examinations depends not only on studying hard but also on planning effectively and managing available time wisely.

The midterm schedule was quite structured, starting from 7th April 2026 and continuing until 15th April 2026. All exams were conducted in Lecture Room EE13, which became a familiar place for us during this intense period. Initially, only Saturday and Sunday were holidays, but due to increased petrol prices, an extra holiday on Friday was announced, giving us additional preparation time.

The extra holiday proved beneficial because it allowed students to revise difficult topics and organize study plans more effectively. Many students used the time to review notes, solve past papers, and discuss important concepts with classmates.

We mostly prepared in groups with friends, which made studying both effective and enjoyable. Group study sessions helped us revise concepts, solve problems, clarify misunderstandings, and stay motivated during stressful moments. Whenever someone struggled with a topic, another friend would often explain it from a different perspective, making learning easier and more interactive.

The first exam was Object-Oriented Programming. Although I had prepared well, the paper was mostly output-based, requiring careful analysis of code snippets and program execution. Unfortunately, I made a mistake in question selection and lost around five marks. Although the result was disappointing, it taught me an important lesson about reading questions carefully and planning answers before attempting them.

The Calculus II exam also included a quiz and covered a wide syllabus, including limits, continuity, differentiation techniques, the chain rule, partial derivatives, gradient vectors, and double integrals. Because the subject involved both theory and calculations, preparation required extensive practice. I revised regularly and solved multiple examples before the exam. Alhamdulillah, the paper went well and boosted my confidence.

Applied Statistics was comparatively smooth. The syllabus covered graphical representation of data and statistical measures such as mean, median, mode, variance, and standard deviation. Since I had practiced similar questions before, I felt comfortable during the examination. There was no quiz, and overall the paper went well.

### Challenges, Time Management, and Unexpected Situations

After a short break, we had the Database Systems midterm. The paper pattern was already shared and included SQL queries, theoretical concepts, numerical questions, and a quiz. However, the biggest challenge was time management because all sections had to be completed within a limited time.

During preparation, an unexpected issue occurred. While studying SQL queries with friends, we accidentally damaged a key on a laptop that we were using for practice. What initially seemed like a small issue turned into a lengthy distraction. We spent nearly three to five hours trying different solutions to fix the problem. Unfortunately, this reduced the time available for revision and affected our preparation schedule.

The next day, we attempted to get the laptop repaired, but due to time limitations and travel constraints, it was not possible. Although the issue was frustrating, it taught us that unexpected situations can arise at any time, especially during important academic periods.

Because of this interruption, our preparation was not as strong as originally planned. During the Database Systems examination, I could not complete the paper within the available time and had to leave some questions unanswered, resulting in a loss of approximately 5–8 marks. This experience clearly demonstrated the importance of time management alongside preparation.

Apart from the challenge of completing the paper within the available time, the Database Systems midterm also made me realize how important conceptual understanding is. Throughout the semester, Dr. Bilal Ahmad encouraged us to focus on understanding the logic behind SQL queries rather than simply memorizing syntax. During lectures and discussions, he emphasized that databases are not only about commands but also about understanding relationships between data, designing efficient structures, and solving practical information management problems.

Because of this approach, my preparation was not limited to revising notes. I spent time practicing SQL queries, understanding joins, aggregate functions, grouping operations, and database normalization concepts. These topics initially seemed difficult, but repeated practice gradually improved my confidence. The more I worked on them, the more I realized how important databases are in modern software systems and applications.

One aspect of the course that I appreciated was the emphasis on understanding concepts rather than memorization. This approach helped develop problem-solving skills and encouraged deeper learning. It also made exam preparation more meaningful because the focus shifted from remembering commands to understanding why and how they are used.

Preparing for midterms with friends created a positive learning environment. Whenever one of us struggled with a query or concept, another person would explain it from a different perspective. Sometimes a simple discussion could save hours of confusion. These collaborative study sessions not only improved our understanding but also reduced exam stress. Looking back, many concepts became easier because of teamwork and shared learning.

Although I lost some marks in the Database Systems paper due to time limitations, the experience itself was valuable. It taught me that exam performance depends on more than knowledge alone. Speed, planning, question selection, and time allocation are equally important. A student may know the answers, but without proper time management, achieving the desired result becomes difficult. This lesson stayed with me and influenced the way I prepared for future assessments.

The next exam was Digital Logic Design. The syllabus included combinational circuits, Boolean algebra, minterms, maxterms, K-maps, and logic simplification techniques. Since I had prepared thoroughly and practiced several examples, the paper went smoothly. Completing this exam successfully gave me a sense of relief because one of the more technical subjects was now behind me.

After this, we faced another challenge. Since I had to leave the university soon, we could not go home with a damaged laptop. Therefore, we decided to travel to Faisalabad to get it repaired. We left in the afternoon, visited the repair shop, and returned late at night. Although the trip was tiring, it was necessary. The experience taught me that student life often involves handling practical problems alongside academic responsibilities.

### Final Exams, Return Journey, and Lessons Learned

The last exam was Communication Skills. Even after returning late from Faisalabad, we continued preparing and studied from approximately 10 PM until 2 AM. The syllabus included communication types, barriers to communication, business message organization, and effective communication techniques. Despite being tired, we remained focused because it was the final paper of the midterm series. Alhamdulillah, the exam went well.

After completing all examinations, we left the university early in the morning on 15th April and traveled back home by train. Although the vacations were short, they provided a much-needed opportunity to rest, spend time with family, and recover from the intense academic schedule.

As part of my learning journey in Database Systems, I also explored some of the academic and professional work of <a href="https://scholar.google.com.au/citations?user=8nZ0jVkAAAAJ&hl=en" target="_blank">Dr. Bilal Ahmad on Google Scholar</a>, who taught us both Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems. Learning about his academic interests helped me appreciate how foundational subjects such as programming and databases contribute to broader areas of computing and technology.

Overall, this midterm phase was a complete experience of learning, challenges, teamwork, and personal growth. From academic pressure to unexpected technical problems and even short trips with friends, everything became part of this memorable journey.

It taught me valuable lessons about time management, careful planning, teamwork, adaptability, and staying calm under pressure. These lessons extend far beyond examinations and will continue to be useful in future academic and professional life. Looking back, the midterm experience was not only about achieving grades but also about developing skills and experiences that contribute to personal growth and long-term success.
