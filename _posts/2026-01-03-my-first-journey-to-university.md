### Preparing for Departure and Saying Goodbye to Home

The journey to university is always a memorable and emotional moment in a student’s life, and mine was no different. In fact, it was one of the most important transitions in my life because it was the first time I was leaving my home to start a completely new phase. Everything felt new and uncertain, yet exciting at the same time. The night before my departure, I carefully prepared all my necessary belongings, including clothes, shoes, documents, and other daily essentials. I checked my bags multiple times to ensure that I had packed everything I would need. Since this was my first experience of living away from home, I did not want to forget anything important.

That evening felt different from ordinary days. Even though I was excited about starting university, there was also a sense of nervousness inside me. I spent time with my family, knowing that it would be quite some time before I returned home again. My parents gave me advice about university life, responsibility, and the importance of focusing on my studies. Their words were simple, but they carried a lot of meaning because I knew they were concerned about me starting a new chapter far from home.

I could hardly sleep that night. My mind was filled with thoughts about the future. I imagined what the university campus would look like, what kind of classmates I would meet, and how different life would be compared to college. At the same time, I wondered whether I would be able to manage everything on my own. These thoughts continued until late at night.

Early in the morning, around 7 a.m., I left my home with my father on a bike and headed toward the bus stand. The weather was calm, and the roads were relatively quiet. Although the distance from my home to the bus stand was not very long, that ride felt unusually emotional. I knew that I was not simply traveling to another city; I was beginning a completely new stage of life.

When the time came to say goodbye, I experienced mixed emotions. There was excitement because I had successfully secured admission and was finally going to start my university journey. However, there was also sadness because I was leaving behind the comfort of home, my family, and the familiar environment in which I had spent my entire life. Watching my father see me off at the bus stand was a moment I still remember clearly. It was a simple moment, but it carried deep emotions that are difficult to express in words.

As the bus started moving, I looked out the window and realized that my university journey had officially begun.

### Traveling Alone and Facing New Challenges

After boarding the bus, I began my journey toward Faisalabad. During the trip, many thoughts came into my mind. This was the first time I was traveling such a distance alone for an important purpose. Unlike family trips or short visits to nearby places, this journey represented independence and responsibility.

The travel itself gave me plenty of time to think. I reflected on the effort I had invested in my studies, the entry tests I had appeared in, and the challenges I had overcome to secure admission. I also thought about the opportunities waiting ahead. University life seemed exciting because it offered a chance to learn new skills, meet people from different backgrounds, and prepare for a professional career.

At the same time, I felt nervous. I had never lived away from my family before, and I had no idea what hostel life would be like. Questions kept appearing in my mind. Would I be able to adjust to a new environment? Would I make good friends? Would I be able to manage my studies effectively? These uncertainties made the journey both exciting and stressful.

Several hours later, I finally arrived at the Faisalabad bus stand. Reaching the city was only part of the journey because the university campus itself was located in Khurrianwala, approximately 23 kilometers away from the main city area. Since I had never visited the campus before, I had very little knowledge about local transportation and routes.

For a few moments, I felt confused about what to do next. Being alone in a new city can be intimidating, especially when you are unfamiliar with the area. Fortunately, I remembered that one of my school friends, Abdul Rehman, had some connections at the university. I contacted him and explained my situation.

He responded immediately and connected me with his uncle, who was studying Electrical Engineering at the same university. His uncle provided detailed guidance regarding the route, transportation options, and the expected fare. His advice proved extremely helpful because it prevented me from making costly mistakes and gave me confidence about how to proceed.

Following his instructions, I found suitable local transport and continued my journey toward the campus. I eventually reached the university after paying around 100 rupees. Although the amount was small, the guidance I received was extremely valuable because it helped me navigate an unfamiliar city without unnecessary stress.

This experience taught me an important lesson. Whenever we face unfamiliar situations, seeking guidance from experienced people can make a significant difference. A simple phone call solved a problem that had initially seemed quite challenging.

### First Impressions and the Beginning of a New Phase

When I finally arrived at the university, I experienced something unexpected. For months, I had imagined what my university would look like. Based on pictures, videos, and stories from other students, I had created a certain image in my mind. However, my first impression was somewhat different from what I had expected.

Since it was near the end of the vacation period, many areas of the campus were not in their best condition. Some buildings appeared less maintained than I had imagined, and certain facilities did not match the expectations I had built over time. Compared to some other universities I had previously visited, the infrastructure initially felt disappointing.

For a brief moment, I questioned whether this was really the place where I would spend the next several years of my life. After investing so much effort into gaining admission, I had expected a more impressive first impression. However, as I spent more time on the campus, my perspective gradually began to change.

I realized that the true value of a university is not determined solely by the appearance of its buildings. What matters most is the education, opportunities, experiences, and personal growth that students gain during their time there. A university is ultimately defined by the knowledge acquired, the friendships formed, and the challenges overcome rather than by physical infrastructure alone.

As I explored the campus further, I began imagining the experiences that awaited me. I would soon attend my first classes, meet new classmates, interact with teachers, and adapt to hostel life. Everything was unfamiliar, yet it also felt like the beginning of an exciting adventure.

This journey marked more than a physical move from one city to another. It symbolized my transition from dependence to independence. For the first time, I would be responsible for managing my daily routine, making decisions on my own, and handling challenges without immediate support from family.

Looking back now, my first journey to university remains one of the most memorable experiences of my life. It was filled with excitement, uncertainty, nervousness, and learning. The emotions I felt during that journey taught me valuable lessons about independence, adaptability, and confidence. It was the first step into a new world of opportunities and challenges that would shape both my academic journey and personal development.

Although many years may pass, I will always remember that early morning departure, the long bus ride, the confusion at the Faisalabad bus stand, the helpful guidance from friends, and the moment I first stepped onto the university campus. Together, these experiences became the foundation of my university life and marked the beginning of an unforgettable chapter in my journey.
