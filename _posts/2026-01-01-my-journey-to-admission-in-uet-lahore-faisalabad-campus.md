### Balancing Board Exams and Entry Test Preparation

My journey toward becoming a Computer Engineering student began during the final phase of my FSc Part-II. It was one of the most important and challenging periods of my academic life because I had to handle multiple responsibilities at the same time. While many students around me were fully focused only on their board exams, I found myself balancing two major challenges together: preparing seriously for my intermediate examinations and, at the same time, getting ready for different university entrance tests. I realized early that securing admission to a well-reputed engineering university was not possible without proper planning, consistent effort, and strong determination. That is why I decided to start my preparation for entry tests alongside my academic studies instead of waiting until the exams were over.

For my preparation, I mainly relied on my FSc textbooks because they provided the basic concepts required for all entry tests. Along with that, I also used different online platforms that offered practice material, past papers, and mock tests. These resources helped me understand the pattern of entry tests and improve my speed and accuracy. This combination of self-study and online practice proved very effective for me. However, managing both board exam preparation and entry test preparation at the same time was not an easy task. There were moments when it became stressful and difficult to maintain focus, but I stayed committed to my goal.

To manage everything efficiently, I created a simple study routine. I tried to give equal importance to both board examinations and entry test preparation. During regular study sessions, I focused on completing my FSc syllabus and understanding concepts thoroughly. After finishing college work, I spent additional time solving MCQs and practicing questions commonly asked in engineering entrance examinations. This routine helped me stay organized and prevented me from falling behind in either area.

As the examination dates came closer, the pressure naturally increased. There were days when I had to spend long hours studying, revising concepts, and solving practice questions. At times, it felt exhausting, but I reminded myself that every hour of effort was bringing me closer to my goal. This period taught me discipline, patience, consistency, and strong time-management skills. More importantly, it taught me how to remain focused even when facing multiple challenges simultaneously. Looking back, I believe this phase played a significant role in preparing me not only for university admission but also for the academic challenges that would come later.

Another important lesson I learned during this period was the value of self-belief. Since engineering admissions are highly competitive, it is easy to compare yourself with other students and feel discouraged. However, I learned to focus on my own progress rather than worrying about what others were doing. By tracking my improvement through practice tests and mock exams, I gained confidence in my abilities. Every small improvement motivated me to work even harder and continue moving forward.

### Building Confidence Through Competitive Test Experience

During this preparation period, I also had to travel to different cities to appear in multiple entry tests. These journeys were not only tiring but also added extra pressure because each test was important for my future. Every examination represented an opportunity to secure admission to a good university, so I approached each one with seriousness and determination. Traveling itself became a learning experience because it required planning, time management, and adaptability.

One of the most significant examinations I attempted was the NUST Entry Test (NET). I appeared in NET twice, and both attempts taught me valuable lessons. During my first attempt, I experienced the competitive testing environment for the first time. Although I had prepared well, I realized there were areas where I could improve. Instead of becoming disappointed, I used the experience as motivation. I carefully analyzed my mistakes, revised weaker topics, and adjusted my preparation strategy. When I appeared for the second time, I felt much more confident and comfortable with the examination format.

In addition to NUST, I also appeared in the UET Taxila entrance test. Each examination had its own style, level of difficulty, and atmosphere. By participating in multiple tests, I became familiar with different testing environments and learned how to manage pressure effectively. Initially, entering large examination halls filled with hundreds of students felt intimidating. However, with each experience, my confidence increased and my anxiety gradually decreased.

One of the biggest benefits of appearing in multiple entrance examinations was that I learned how to perform under pressure. Competitive tests are not only about knowledge; they also require focus, time management, and confidence. Through continuous practice and repeated exposure to examination environments, I became better at handling stress and maintaining concentration throughout the test.

Finally, I appeared in the UET entrance test, which was one of the most important examinations for me. Months of preparation, hard work, and practice had led to this moment. Although I felt nervous before the examination, I also felt prepared. During the test, I focused on managing my time effectively and answering questions carefully. When the examination ended, I felt satisfied because I knew I had given my best effort.

After the test came the waiting period, which felt longer than the preparation itself. Like many students, I frequently checked updates and discussed expected results with friends and family. The anticipation created excitement as well as nervousness. However, I remained hopeful because I knew the effort I had invested throughout the year.

Eventually, the results were announced, and I received the news that I had successfully secured admission to the University of Engineering and Technology (UET), Lahore, Faisalabad Campus, in the field of BSc Computer Engineering. It was one of the happiest moments of my academic life. The achievement represented the reward for months of dedication, persistence, and hard work.

### Beginning University Life at UET Faisalabad

After my admission was confirmed, I visited the main campus in Lahore to submit all the required documents and complete the admission formalities. This step made everything feel more real and official. Until that point, admission had been a goal I was working toward, but after completing the paperwork, it became a reality. Walking through the university environment for the first time as an admitted student was an exciting experience. I felt proud to be part of an institution that had a strong reputation in engineering education.

Shortly after completing the admission process, I moved to the Faisalabad campus to begin my university life. I entered this new phase with a mixture of excitement, curiosity, and a little nervousness. It was a completely new environment where I had to adjust to new people, new surroundings, and a different routine. Everything felt different from college life, and I knew that university would bring new responsibilities and challenges.

One of the biggest changes was adapting to hostel life and living away from home. For the first time, I had to manage many responsibilities independently. Initially, adjusting to this lifestyle was not easy, but it gradually helped me become more responsible and self-reliant. Living in the hostel also gave me the opportunity to meet students from different cities and backgrounds, which broadened my perspective and improved my communication skills.

The first few days at the Faisalabad campus were filled with exploration and new experiences. I spent time becoming familiar with the campus environment, academic departments, classrooms, and facilities. Meeting new classmates and interacting with seniors helped me settle into university life more comfortably. These interactions gave me valuable insights into academic expectations and campus culture.

Looking back, my admission journey was about much more than simply securing a seat in a university. It was a journey of learning, growth, discipline, and self-discovery. The challenges I faced while balancing board examinations, preparing for entry tests, traveling for examinations, and adapting to a new environment helped shape my personality and strengthen my confidence. Securing admission to UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus, was not the end of a journey—it was the beginning of a new chapter filled with opportunities, challenges, friendships, and experiences that would contribute significantly to my personal and academic development.
