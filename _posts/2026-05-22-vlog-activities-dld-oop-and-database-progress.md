### Communication Vlog Activities and Presentation Experience

This week started with an unexpected correction in our Communication Skills class that completely changed our understanding of the vlog assignment. Many students, including me, had misunderstood the activity because we missed one of the earlier lectures and received incomplete instructions from classmates. We originally believed that the task only required standing in one place, recording a video, and speaking on any topic in English.

Because of this misunderstanding, several students prepared simple speaking videos instead of actual vlogs. However, when our teacher checked the submissions during class presentations, she rejected many of them and explained the proper requirements in detail. According to the actual instructions, the activity was supposed to be a real vlog-style presentation where students had to move between different locations, interact naturally with the environment, and explain surroundings while walking and speaking in English.

At first, many students became worried because a lot of effort had already been put into recording the previous videos. Fortunately, the teacher understood the confusion and gave everyone another opportunity to reattempt the task correctly.

For the second attempt, I decided to create a more practical and realistic vlog around **Al-Biruni Hall and the hostel surroundings**. One of my friends helped by handling the camera while I walked through different areas and explained the environment in English. Compared to the first attempt, this experience felt much more interactive and enjoyable because it required confidence, communication skills, and natural speaking instead of simply memorizing lines in front of a camera.

The evaluation day itself became a memorable experience. The classroom atmosphere was arranged almost like a proper screening session. The lights were turned off, videos were displayed using a projector, and speakers were connected for clear audio. Students watched each other’s recordings while the teacher evaluated presentation quality, confidence, communication style, and creativity.

Alhamdulillah, my vlog presentation went well, and I received **8 out of 10 marks** in the activity. This gave me confidence because public speaking and communication in English are areas that improve gradually through practice and exposure.

At the end of the class, the teacher announced another communication activity in which every student would become a teacher for 10 minutes. Each student had to explain a topic in front of the class and answer questions from classmates. This assignment seemed both challenging and interesting because it would test speaking confidence, topic understanding, and interaction with the audience at the same time.

### Database Project Progress, SQL Quiz, and University Activities

Alongside communication activities, this week was also extremely important for our Database Systems course because our group finally finalized the dataset for our semester project after facing rejection earlier.

Initially, selecting a suitable dataset was difficult because the teacher wanted a meaningful and research-oriented dataset instead of a simple or random collection of records. After searching and discussing different options, we finally selected the **Framingham Heart Study dataset**, which is a real-world medical dataset widely used for heart disease prediction research.

The dataset contained approximately 4,240 patient records with 16 different attributes. One of the most important fields was the target variable named `TenYearCHD`, which predicts whether a patient may develop coronary heart disease within the next ten years.

While studying this dataset, we learned many practical concepts related to data analysis and Machine Learning preparation. We observed how real datasets contain missing values, class imbalance issues, and complex relationships between features. We also understood the importance of recall in medical prediction systems because false predictions in healthcare-related systems can become extremely sensitive and dangerous.

Our teacher appreciated the dataset because it was realistic, research-based, and suitable for future Machine Learning work. This approval was important for our project because earlier rejections had already delayed our progress.

Working on the Framingham Heart Study dataset helped us understand that database projects involve much more than storing information. Dataset selection, data quality, target variables, and practical problem-solving all play an important role in developing meaningful projects and future Machine Learning applications.

During project discussions, Dr. Bilal Ahmad encouraged students to think critically about dataset selection and focus on solving real-world problems rather than choosing random datasets. This approach helped us better understand the connection between database systems, data analysis, and predictive modeling.

As part of exploring academic resources beyond classroom lectures, I also reviewed the <a href="https://scholar.google.com.au/citations?user=8nZ0jVkAAAAJ&hl=en" target="_blank">academic profile of Dr. Bilal Ahmad</a>, who taught us both Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems. Learning about the broader academic side of computing helped me appreciate how foundational subjects contribute to advanced areas of technology and research.

During the same period, we also attempted a Database quiz based on relational algebra and SQL concepts. The quiz included topics such as Selection, Projection, Rename, Union, Intersection, Difference, Division, and Joins. Instead of focusing only on theory, the questions were designed around practical scenarios involving Current Account, Saving Account, Student, Course, and Enrollment tables.

We solved problems involving data retrieval, common record identification, exclusive record filtering, and relational joins. This quiz helped strengthen my practical understanding of relational databases and SQL query implementation because we had to apply concepts logically instead of simply memorizing definitions.

Despite the academic workload, the university environment also included enjoyable moments during the week. On 19 May 2026, a Mela festival was organized on campus where students arranged different stalls related to food, games, entertainment, and small business activities. The environment became lively and energetic because students from different departments participated actively.

There were Golgappa stalls, Ludo game setups, artificial jewelry stalls, and several food and entertainment activities across the university area. After continuous academic pressure from labs, quizzes, and projects, the festival provided students with an opportunity to relax and spend enjoyable time with friends.

Interestingly, the same day also became memorable for another reason. In the morning, I accidentally dropped my mobile phone, and its panel came out. At first, I became worried because phones are extremely important for communication, online classes, assignments, and university coordination. Later, my friend Tahir and I travelled to Khurrianwala to repair the phone. Although it started as a stressful situation, the short journey itself became another memorable experience during this busy academic week.

### DLD Open-Ended Lab, OOP Responsibilities, and Academic Pressure

At the same time, our Digital Logic Design open-ended lab created one of the most challenging academic situations of the week.

Before the lab session, our teacher instructed us to revise K-map concepts, IC numbers, pin diagrams, logic circuit design, and breadboard implementation carefully. Because of these instructions, many students believed that the evaluation would mainly focus on theoretical understanding and memorization of IC information.

However, when the lab actually started, the teacher surprised everyone by presenting a completely real-world scenario-based problem instead of direct theoretical questions. The system design involved multiple conditions such as high temperature, vibration detection, emergency situations, and sound detection.

We were required to analyze the scenario, identify inputs and outputs, create Boolean expressions using K-maps, draw proper logic diagrams, and finally implement the complete circuit practically on a breadboard.

Initially, many students became confused because the problem appeared complex and unfamiliar. Since it was an open-ended scenario, there was no direct formula or predefined method available. After carefully understanding the conditions, I identified the variables properly and began constructing the truth table systematically.

I used variables such as `E` for Emergency and `V` for Vibration while assigning other variables for remaining conditions. Since the system involved four variables, the truth table contained sixteen possible combinations, making the simplification process more detailed.

After completing the K-map simplification, we implemented the logic practically on the breadboard. Alhamdulillah, our circuit worked correctly during testing, and I also performed confidently during the viva. The teacher checked the implementation, verified the outputs, and appreciated the proper working of the circuit. This lab became an important practical learning experience because it tested problem-solving ability rather than simple memorization.

Along with DLD activities, our Object-Oriented Programming work also continued actively throughout the week. Our previously assigned **University Admission Management System** project was submitted successfully before the deadline. Shortly afterwards, the teacher introduced another mini project where we had to design a simple 2D game in C# using the .NET Framework without relying on advanced game engines or frameworks.

In the same lab session, we also attempted quizzes and open-ended tasks based on inheritance, polymorphism, abstract classes, and object creation in C#. One activity required us to create an abstract class named `Product` along with derived classes such as `Clothing` and `Electronics`. We also had to demonstrate object functionality and proper implementation inside the main class.

During the same week, our DLD teacher also announced quiz and midterm results. Alhamdulillah, I secured **9.5 out of 10** in the quiz and **25 out of 30** in the midterm examination. Another quiz from Chapter 4 was also announced, which meant that academic preparation would continue even during the upcoming vacation period.

During this same period, the university administration announced that physical classes would temporarily shift online from 20 May due to the upcoming Eid-ul-Adha vacations. We attended online classes on 21st, 22nd, and 25th May, while holidays continued afterwards until the university reopened physically on 1st June.

Because of the sudden schedule change, many students immediately started preparing to travel back home. After finishing our OOP lab session, we quickly packed our luggage and travelled towards the railway station using the university route bus.

The journey itself felt tiring because the week had already been filled with quizzes, labs, projects, assignments, and practical evaluations. I reached my Mamu’s home at around 10:20 p.m., stayed there overnight, and finally reached my own home the next morning at approximately 7:50 a.m.

What made the situation more interesting was that my first online lecture was scheduled at 11 a.m. the same day. After reaching home, there was very little time left to rest before classes resumed again in online mode.

I wrote this article on Monday, 21 May 2026, shortly before attending that lecture.

Overall, this week became a combination of communication activities, technical learning, practical implementation, project responsibilities, travel, and time management. From improving English speaking confidence and database understanding to handling challenging DLD labs and academic pressure, every experience contributed to my personal growth and practical learning journey as a Computer Engineering student.
