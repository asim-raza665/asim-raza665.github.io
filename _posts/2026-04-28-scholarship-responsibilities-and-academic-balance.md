### Scholarship Responsibilities and Time Management

After midterm exams, our regular classes resumed on 15th April. Just a few days later, on 20th April, I received an exciting update that I had been selected for the CM Honours Scholarship. It was a proud moment for me, but at the same time, it came with urgent responsibilities.

The deadline to submit the required documents was 22nd April, which gave me very limited time. Along with basic documents, I also had to submit a deed of agreement that required official signatures and stamps. Since this process could not be completed at the university, I initially planned to travel back home.

At the same time, I had a Database Systems lab scheduled for the next day. The lab was expected to end at 4:00 PM, while my train time was 4:30 PM. I discussed this situation with my teacher, explaining the urgency of my scholarship work. Thankfully, my teacher understood and allowed me to complete my task early. After checking my work and confirming my train timing, he evaluated my marks and gave me permission to leave.

The lab task was based on normalization, where we had to:

- Convert unnormalized data into First Normal Form (1NF)  
- Then transform it into Second Normal Form (2NF)  
- Implement the solution using MySQL Workbench  

This lab helped us understand how to organize data efficiently and reduce redundancy.

Before leaving, I discussed my situation with one of my school teachers. I explained that I needed signatures and stamps from authorized officers. He kindly offered help and told me to send the documents to his home so he could arrange everything.

I then contacted my father and asked him to print the documents on stamp paper and deliver them to my teacher. Because of their support, I cancelled my travel plan. This decision saved both time and energy and showed me the importance of communication, trust, and having supportive people around me.

### Communication Skills Activities and Confidence Building

After the weekend, we had a Communication Skills lab on Monday. The task was to perform a talk show in pairs, where one student would act as the host and the other as the guest.

Me and my friend Tahir selected the topic:  
**“Role of Social Media in Student Life.”**

- Tahir acted as the host  
- I performed as the guest  

We started our talk show confidently, discussed different aspects of social media, and shared real-life examples. At the end, both students and the teacher asked us questions, which we answered effectively.

We scored **4 out of 5 marks**, which was a good result and a valuable experience in improving our communication and confidence.

After this activity, our teacher assigned a new continuous task:

- Record a **10-minute video daily**  
- Speak in **English**  
- Choose a **vlog or any random topic**  
- Submit it **every day until final exams**  

This task was challenging but very helpful in building speaking skills, confidence, and consistency.


### Unexpected Quizzes and Learning Outcomes

The very next day, we faced two surprise quizzes in Calculus and Statistics. However, the Calculus quiz did not go well for most students. Many students scored below average, and some even received zero marks.

Because of this, the teacher decided to cancel the quiz and announced that it would be rescheduled after two days.

On the other hand, the Statistics quiz was comparatively better, as it included example-based questions that were easier to attempt.

This phase of my academic journey was full of responsibilities, quick decisions, and new challenges. From managing scholarship deadlines to handling lab work, improving communication skills, and facing unexpected quizzes, every moment contributed to my personal and academic growth.

I learned:

- How to manage time under pressure  
- The importance of communication and asking for help  
- How to adapt quickly to changing situations  
- The value of consistency in skill development  

Overall, this experience was not just about academics but also about handling real-life responsibilities alongside university life.
