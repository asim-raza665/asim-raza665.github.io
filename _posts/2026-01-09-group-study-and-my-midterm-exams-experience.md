### Transition from Solo Study to Group Learning

After settling into my daily routine and becoming more familiar with university life, the next significant phase of my journey was preparing for quizzes and midterm exams. Initially, the concept of group study was completely new to me. During my FSc studies, I was used to studying alone, relying mostly on personal notes and self-practice. I preferred finding a quiet place, reading the material, and solving problems independently. This method had worked reasonably well for me before university, so I naturally continued using the same approach during the early weeks of my first semester.

However, at UET Faisalabad, I quickly realized that university-level studies were different. The courses were more challenging, the concepts were deeper, and the pace of learning was much faster than what I had experienced before. Simply reading notes was often not enough. There were times when I understood a topic partially but still had questions that I could not answer on my own. That was when I began to appreciate the value of learning with others.

I formed a study group with my closest friends, including my best friend Tahir Yaqoob, as well as other classmates from the hostel and our courses. We made it a habit to meet regularly and discuss whatever topics we found difficult. Sometimes we gathered in hostel rooms, while other times we sat together in available study spaces. Instead of competing with each other, we focused on helping one another understand concepts better.

These sessions became much more than simple study meetings. We exchanged notes, solved questions together, compared our approaches to problems, and explained topics to each other whenever someone was confused. Many times, a concept that seemed difficult when studied alone became much easier after a group discussion. Different students had different strengths, and this diversity benefited everyone.

Subjects like Programming Fundamentals, Calculus, and Discrete Mathematics often required detailed understanding rather than memorization. During group discussions, we would break down complex topics into smaller parts and explain them in simple language. Sometimes one friend understood a mathematical concept better, while another had a stronger understanding of programming logic. This collaborative learning environment helped all of us improve together.

One of the biggest benefits of group study was motivation. When studying alone, it is easy to become distracted or postpone difficult tasks. In a group, everyone encouraged each other to remain focused. Seeing friends work hard naturally motivated me to put in more effort as well. Over time, these study sessions became an important part of my academic routine and helped me build confidence before quizzes and exams.

### Midterm Preparation, Quizzes, and Academic Improvement

As midterm exams approached, our focus became more serious. Although the syllabus covered in the midterms was moderate compared to final exams, I wanted to perform well because these exams would indicate how successfully I was adapting to university studies. I knew that strong midterm results would increase my confidence for the rest of the semester.

Among all subjects, Programming Fundamentals required the most attention from me. Since I came from a non-computer background, learning programming was not easy during the initial weeks. Understanding coding logic, syntax, conditions, loops, and problem-solving techniques required a completely different way of thinking. Sometimes I would spend a long time trying to understand why a program was not working correctly, only to discover that a small mistake was causing the entire issue.

Our Programming Fundamentals course was taught by **Dr. Bilal Ahmad**, whose academic interests include Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning, and whose teaching style encouraged students to focus on understanding rather than memorization. He often reminded us that programming is not about remembering code line by line; it is about developing logical thinking and problem-solving skills. This advice became especially useful during midterm preparation because I realized that simply memorizing examples would not help me solve new questions in the exam.

During lectures and discussions, Dr. Bilal Ahmad frequently emphasized the importance of practical learning. He encouraged students to think beyond marks and focus on building strong technical foundations. Because of his interest in AI, Machine Learning (ML), and Deep Learning (DL), he occasionally connected basic programming concepts to larger real-world applications. These discussions helped me understand that Programming Fundamentals was not just another course but the starting point for many advanced computing fields.

Another interesting point he often highlighted was the importance of quality data in technology applications. He discussed how machine learning systems depend on accurate datasets and mentioned that medical datasets are often valuable because they are collected with high precision and accuracy. Although these topics were beyond the scope of our first-semester syllabus, they helped students appreciate the practical significance of programming skills.

Initially, my quiz results were moderate, which reflected my early struggles. For example, I scored 7 out of 10 in one quiz and only 4 to 5 marks in another. At first, these scores were disappointing because I expected better performance. However, instead of becoming discouraged, I treated them as feedback. They helped me identify weaknesses and showed me where improvement was needed.

The group study sessions became even more valuable during this period. We solved programming problems together, discussed assignments, and reviewed important concepts before quizzes. Whenever someone made a mistake, the group would analyze it and explain the correct approach. This process helped me learn much faster than studying alone. Gradually, my understanding improved, and I became more confident in solving problems independently.

As the exam dates approached, our daily routine became more organized. We allocated time for revision, practice questions, and discussions. Rather than relying on last-minute preparation, we tried to review concepts consistently. Looking back, this structured preparation played a major role in improving my performance.

### Exam Results and Lessons from Collaborative Learning

When the midterm exam results were announced, they were very encouraging. The effort invested in preparation, practice, and group study had finally produced positive results. Seeing the outcome of weeks of hard work was both satisfying and motivating.

In Programming Fundamentals, I scored **37 out of 40**, which was a significant improvement compared to my earlier quiz performance. Considering the difficulties I faced during the first few weeks of learning programming, this result felt like a major achievement. It showed me that consistent practice and determination could overcome initial weaknesses.

I also performed well in my other subjects. My results included **Calculus 28 out of 30, ICT 26 out of 30, Discrete Mathematics 24.5 out of 30, Physics 24 out of 30, and Fehm ul Quran 22 out of 30**. These scores reflected the effort I had invested throughout the preparation period and gave me confidence that I was progressing in the right direction.

More importantly, these results taught me that academic success is rarely the result of a single factor. Individual effort was important, but it was not the only reason for improvement. Support from friends, effective teaching from instructors, and collaborative learning all played significant roles. The experience showed me that learning becomes much more effective when students work together and share knowledge openly.

The midterm period also taught me valuable lessons about planning and consistency. Strong results did not come from a single night of studying; they came from weeks of preparation, practice, and discussion. Every study session, every solved problem, and every reviewed topic contributed to the final outcome. This realization changed the way I viewed exams and academic preparation.

Another lesson I learned was the importance of asking questions. In the beginning, students sometimes hesitate to admit when they do not understand something. However, group study created an environment where questions were welcomed. Whenever one person asked a question, several others often benefited from the explanation. This made learning more efficient and less stressful.

Overall, the midterm exams were much more than a test of knowledge. They represented a turning point in my first semester. Through this experience, I learned the value of teamwork, preparation, persistence, and continuous improvement. I also discovered that success becomes easier when people support each other and work toward common goals.

The confidence I gained from these results motivated me to continue working hard in the remainder of the semester. More importantly, the experience taught me that university education is not only about individual achievement. It is also about collaboration, shared learning, and personal growth. The lessons I learned during this period remained useful throughout my academic journey and helped shape the study habits that I continue to use today.
