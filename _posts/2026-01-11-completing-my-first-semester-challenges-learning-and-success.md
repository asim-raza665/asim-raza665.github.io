### Preparing for Final Exams and Building Study Discipline

The end of my first semester at UET Faisalabad Campus was both challenging and rewarding. After completing the midterm exams, the next major task was preparing for the final exams, which required comprehensive revision of all the concepts covered throughout the semester, including topics taught before the midterms. For me, this period was particularly demanding because it involved balancing multiple responsibilities, maintaining a consistent study routine, managing hostel life, and, whenever possible, spending quality time with my family. It became clear that effective time management and self-discipline were essential if I wanted to cover all subjects thoroughly, from Programming Fundamentals and Calculus to Physics, ICT, and Discrete Mathematics.

Unlike midterms, where only part of the syllabus was included, final exams required a complete understanding of everything taught throughout the semester. This meant revisiting old topics while continuing to prepare for newly completed chapters. At times, the workload felt overwhelming because every subject demanded attention. I quickly realized that randomly studying different topics would not be effective. Instead, I needed a proper plan.

To manage this challenge, I created a study schedule and divided my available time according to the difficulty of each subject. Some subjects required concept revision, while others needed extensive problem-solving practice. Programming Fundamentals required special attention because coding skills improve through practice rather than memorization. I spent extra time revising Python concepts, solving programming problems, and reviewing previous lab exercises.

Our Programming Fundamentals course was taught by **Dr. Bilal Ahmad**, and many of the lessons he emphasized throughout the semester became particularly useful during final exam preparation. He consistently encouraged students to focus on understanding concepts rather than memorizing code. While preparing for finals, I realized how important this advice was because programming questions often required logical thinking and problem-solving rather than recalling specific examples.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad frequently highlighted the importance of strong programming fundamentals for future learning. Because of his interest in Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), and Deep Learning (DL), he often encouraged students to think about how basic programming concepts could eventually support more advanced technologies. He also discussed how real-world machine learning applications depend heavily on high-quality datasets and meaningful problem-solving. One example he often mentioned was the use of medical datasets because they are usually collected with a high degree of precision and accuracy. These discussions motivated me to take programming more seriously and helped me understand the practical value of what we were learning.

During this intense period, I developed several new study habits that proved crucial for my academic success. I began making detailed notes and summaries for each subject, which allowed me to revise efficiently before exams. Regular practice of problems, especially in Programming Fundamentals and Calculus, helped reinforce my understanding of difficult concepts. I also learned that consistent daily revision was much more effective than trying to study large amounts of material at the last moment. These habits not only improved my preparation for finals but also became useful practices that I continued using in later semesters.

### Group Study and the Machine Learning Final Project Experience

Group study sessions with my friends became a key part of my preparation. My closest friends, particularly Tahir Yaqoob, along with other classmates, played an important role in keeping me motivated and focused. During these sessions, we discussed difficult topics, solved problems collaboratively, and shared different approaches to learning. This experience taught me the value of teamwork and how peer support can enhance understanding and retention of knowledge.

The examination period was busy, but one of the most exciting parts of the semester was our final project in Programming Fundamentals. This project gave us an opportunity to apply many of the concepts we had learned during the semester in a practical setting. Unlike regular assignments, the project required teamwork, research, problem-solving, and independent learning.

One of the most exciting and memorable moments of the semester was our final project in Programming Fundamentals. The project was based on creating a Machine Learning model using provided datasets. My group, which included my roommate Muhammad Umair, chose to work on a Cardiovascular Disease dataset. At the start, I felt completely unprepared because I had no prior experience in Python or machine learning. A few months earlier, I was still struggling with basic programming concepts, and now I was participating in a project involving data analysis and machine learning.

Although the project initially seemed intimidating, we approached it step by step. Through three days of focused effort, dedicated learning, and applying what I had learned in class, we successfully created and presented our model. The project taught me that even unfamiliar challenges can be managed when broken into smaller tasks.

This project focused on developing a supervised machine learning model for predicting cardiovascular disease using lifestyle and health-related data. We implemented a complete pipeline in Python, starting from data preprocessing and cleaning, where we handled missing values and removed unrealistic health records such as abnormal blood pressure values. We performed feature engineering by converting age into meaningful units and organizing it into different groups for better understanding.

To explore the dataset, we created multiple visualizations such as age distribution, cholesterol levels, and relationships between blood pressure and cardiovascular risk, which helped us gain deeper insights into the data. This stage of the project was particularly interesting because it showed how data can reveal patterns that are difficult to observe directly.

We then trained multiple machine learning models, including Decision Tree, Logistic Regression, and Random Forest classifiers. After evaluating their performance using accuracy scores, classification reports, and confusion matrices, we selected Random Forest as the best-performing model. Additionally, we implemented probability-based predictions to estimate the likelihood of cardiovascular disease, making the model more practical and informative.

Looking back, I now realize why Dr. Bilal Ahmad often discussed real-world datasets and practical applications. Working with a medical dataset allowed me to see how programming and data analysis could contribute to meaningful problems. The project was not just about obtaining marks; it demonstrated how technical skills can be applied to analyze information and support decision-making. This experience strengthened my interest in programming and introduced me to the broader possibilities available in computing fields.

### Results, CGPA Achievement, and Semester Reflection

The moment we saw our project working was immensely satisfying. After investing significant time and effort into learning new concepts, debugging code, and improving the model, seeing successful results felt rewarding. Our presentation went well, and we scored **9 out of 12 marks**, which boosted my confidence and reassured me that my programming skills were gradually improving. This experience marked a major milestone in my academic journey because, just a few months earlier, coding had been entirely new to me.

The completion of the project also gave me a sense of achievement beyond academic grades. It showed me that learning difficult skills becomes possible when approached with patience, persistence, and consistent effort. Every challenge I had faced during the semester contributed to this moment, from struggling with basic Python syntax to successfully participating in a machine learning project.

When the final results were announced, all my hard work paid off. I achieved a **CGPA of 3.83**, secured **third position in my section**, and achieved **fourth position overall across both sections**. This achievement was one of the proudest moments of my first semester because it reflected not only academic performance but also personal growth and determination.

Receiving these results reminded me of how much progress I had made since the first day of university. During the beginning of the semester, I was adapting to a new environment, adjusting to hostel life, learning unfamiliar subjects, and building new study habits. By the end of the semester, I had successfully completed my courses, strengthened my technical skills, worked on a machine learning project, and achieved strong academic results.

Reflecting on my first semester, I realized how much I had developed in multiple areas. I learned Python programming, improved my problem-solving abilities, gained experience working in teams, completed my first machine learning project, adjusted to hostel life, and cultivated disciplined study habits. These experiences were just as valuable as the grades themselves because they helped shape my approach toward learning and personal development.

Overall, my first semester at UET Faisalabad Campus was a journey filled with challenges, learning experiences, and success. It taught me important lessons in consistency, collaboration, discipline, and perseverance. The support of friends, the guidance of teachers, and the effort I invested throughout the semester all contributed to this achievement. Most importantly, the semester gave me confidence in my ability to succeed in Computer Engineering and prepared me to face future academic challenges with determination and a positive mindset. Looking back, I consider this semester an important foundation for everything that followed in my university journey.

Since our Programming Fundamentals project introduced me to machine learning concepts for the first time, I also explored some of the research work shared through Dr. Bilal Ahmad's [Google Scholar profile](https://scholar.google.com.au/citations?user=8nZ0jVkAAAAJ&hl=en). It helped me understand how foundational programming skills can contribute to AI, Machine Learning, and data-driven problem solving.
