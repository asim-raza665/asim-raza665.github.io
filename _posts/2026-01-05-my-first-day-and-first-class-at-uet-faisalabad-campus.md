### Preparing for the First Academic Experience

After settling into hostel life, the next major milestone in my university journey was my very first day of classes at UET Faisalabad Campus. Unlike the previous few days, which were mostly spent adjusting to the hostel environment and exploring the campus, this day felt much more important because it marked the official beginning of my academic life as a university student.

The night before my first class was filled with excitement and curiosity. Since everything was new, I spent a lot of time thinking about what university classes would be like. I wondered about the teachers, the subjects, the classrooms, and the students I would meet. At the same time, I felt slightly nervous because I had no idea what to expect. School and college had familiar routines, but university was an entirely different world.

I woke up early that morning with a sense of anticipation. After taking a shower and getting ready, I carefully organized my bag. At that point, I did not have many study materials with me. I packed a register, a pen, a few markers, and some basic stationery items that I thought might be useful. Since I had never attended a university class before, I was unsure about what exactly would be required, but I wanted to be as prepared as possible.

As I left the hostel and walked toward the academic area, I observed everything around me very carefully. Students were moving in different directions, some confidently walking toward their departments while others appeared just as confused as I was. The campus looked much more active than before because classes were officially starting. Watching senior students interact with each other made me realize that they had already become part of this environment, while I was only beginning my journey.

During that walk, I experienced a mixture of confidence and nervousness. On the outside, I tried to appear calm and composed, but internally I was thinking about many things. I wondered whether I would understand the lectures, whether I would make friends in class, and whether I would be able to meet the academic expectations of a university-level program. Despite these uncertainties, there was also excitement because I knew that I was finally beginning the educational journey I had worked so hard to achieve.

The atmosphere of the campus on that first morning remains one of my strongest memories. Everything felt new, and every small detail seemed important. It was the moment when I truly felt that my university life had begun.

### First Classes, Confusion, and Early Learning Experience

The first challenge of the day appeared even before attending a class. Like many new students, we did not know the exact location of our department. We had the timetable, but finding the correct building and classroom was another matter entirely. Several groups of freshmen were walking around trying to understand the campus layout.

For a few minutes, confusion spread among us as we tried to figure out where to go. Eventually, we approached some senior students and asked for directions. They guided us toward our department, and after a little searching, we finally managed to locate the correct building. Although it may seem like a small achievement now, at that moment it felt like a significant accomplishment because it gave us confidence that we could start navigating the university independently.

Before classes officially started, many of us gathered together to discuss the timetable. We looked at the list of subjects, classroom numbers, and instructor names. Some students were trying to understand abbreviations, while others were discussing what each subject might involve. Since we were all new to the university system, everything seemed slightly confusing at first.

My first class, as far as I remember, was Calculus. Walking into the classroom for the first time was an interesting experience. The atmosphere felt more serious and professional than anything I had experienced in college. The instructor entered the classroom, introduced the course, and immediately began discussing academic expectations. It became clear that university education would require greater responsibility and self-discipline than previous stages of education.

One thing that immediately stood out was the teaching style. Unlike college, where teachers often guided students step by step through every topic, university instructors expected students to take more responsibility for their own learning. This realization made me understand that success at the university level would require independent study and continuous effort.

Later in the day, we attended our Programming Fundamentals lab. This was particularly exciting because it was my first formal exposure to programming and computer science concepts. Up until that point, I had no practical experience with programming languages. Everything seemed unfamiliar, including the software, terminology, and procedures being discussed.

During that first lab session, our primary task was to install Python on our systems. The activity itself was simple, but because I had never worked with programming tools before, even this basic task felt completely new. I followed the instructions provided by the instructor and completed the installation process along with my classmates.

Although I successfully completed the task, I did not fully understand the significance of Python or how it would be used in future classes. At that stage, I was simply following instructions and trying to keep up with the new environment. Nevertheless, that lab marked the beginning of my journey into the world of programming, a subject that would later become an important part of my academic development.

### Realization of University Life and Memorable First Day Events

One of the most memorable moments of the day occurred during the Programming Fundamentals lecture. While taking attendance, the instructor called my roll number and then casually asked what we had done in the lab session earlier. Since the only thing I clearly remembered was installing Python, I confidently replied, "We installed Python."

The instructor then asked a few follow-up questions related to the installation and the purpose of the software. Unfortunately, I could not answer them because I genuinely did not understand the concepts yet. For a moment, I felt embarrassed and nervous. Sitting in front of an entire class without being able to answer simple questions was uncomfortable.

However, that moment became an important lesson for me. It made me realize that university education is not just about attending classes or following instructions. It requires understanding concepts, asking questions, and actively engaging with the material being taught. Unlike FSc studies, where many topics relied on memorization and repetition, university learning demanded deeper comprehension and practical application.

After the academic sessions ended, another memorable experience awaited us outside the classroom. During a break, a group of seniors approached some of the new students, including me. Their interaction was mostly light-hearted, but for freshers, it felt slightly intimidating because we were still adjusting to the new environment.

They asked us to introduce ourselves, share information about our hometowns, and explain why we had chosen our respective fields of study. Since I was already somewhat nervous from the events of the day, speaking in front of seniors felt challenging. At one point, they even asked me to say the complete name of the university in Urdu and repeat my introduction several times while standing on a chair.

Although I felt uncomfortable at that moment, the seniors were not being hostile. Looking back now, I realize that they were simply interacting with newcomers in a traditional university manner. What felt intimidating then eventually became a funny memory that I often recall with a smile.

After spending an entire day attending classes, exploring the campus, meeting teachers, and interacting with seniors, I finally returned to the hostel. I was physically tired but mentally energized because so many new experiences had taken place within a single day.

After having lunch, I spent some time resting and reflecting on everything that had happened. As I thought about the events of the day, one realization became very clear: university life was completely different from school and college life. It demanded greater independence, confidence, adaptability, and responsibility. Success would require more than simply attending lectures; it would require active learning, continuous effort, and the willingness to step outside my comfort zone.

That first day was filled with confusion, excitement, nervousness, learning, and memorable encounters. More importantly, it marked the beginning of a journey that would shape my academic career and personal growth. Even today, when I look back on my university experience, I remember that first day as the moment when I truly understood that a new chapter of my life had begun.
