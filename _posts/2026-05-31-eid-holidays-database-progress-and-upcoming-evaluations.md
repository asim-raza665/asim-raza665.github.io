### Holiday Responsibilities, Academic Planning, and Communication Preparation

After the university shifted to online mode due to the Eid-ul-Adha holidays, students temporarily moved away from regular campus routines. On 24th May, no online classes were conducted, which gave us a completely free day. Since I also had some urgent work related to my father, I travelled to Gujrat to handle those responsibilities.

Although there were no classes, university work continued through announcements and upcoming deadlines. During the holidays, our Digital Logic Design teacher shared the complete schedule of upcoming academic activities. We were informed that manual submission and binding would take place on 1st June, followed by a lab quiz on 3rd June, a project exhibition on 8th June, and Quiz No. 2 from Chapter 4 on 10th June.

As soon as these dates were announced, many students, including me, started planning how to prepare for all these evaluations. The holidays no longer felt completely free because several important assessments were approaching immediately after the university reopened.

At the same time, our Communication Skills presentation was also getting closer. In the previous class, our teacher had assigned every student a 10-minute teaching presentation in which we had to act like a teacher and explain a topic in front of the class while answering questions from students.

Since the activity carried 10 marks, I wanted to prepare properly. After considering different topics, I decided to select **Binary Conversion** because it is one of the fundamental concepts in computing and is directly related to our field.

I prepared a presentation consisting of nine slides. In these slides, I covered how decimal numbers are converted into binary form and how binary numbers are converted back into decimal. Along with the conversion methods, I also explained why computers use binary language instead of the decimal system and how digital devices process information using only two states.

Preparing this presentation helped me revise basic computer concepts while also improving my confidence for public speaking. Since the presentation was scheduled for 1st June after the university reopened physically, I spent part of the holidays reviewing the slides and practicing explanations that would be easy for classmates to understand.

### Database Project Development and Lab Manual Preparation

During the holidays, a large portion of my time was dedicated to our Database Systems project. As mentioned in my previous article, our group had already finalized the **Framingham Heart Study dataset**, and the next phase involved designing a proper database structure before moving towards future Machine Learning implementation.

Our teacher provided instructions regarding database design and explained that we should continue working on the project during the online lecture period. Since the dataset contained thousands of records and many attributes, designing the database manually required considerable effort.

To simplify the process, I used AI assistance to better understand the dataset structure. After sharing the dataset and discussing the requirements, I generated SQL queries that allowed me to create a database containing all the input fields from the dataset. Afterwards, I applied normalization concepts and worked on converting the design into a more organized structure based on First Normal Form, Second Normal Form, and Third Normal Form.

This activity helped me connect theoretical database concepts with practical implementation. Instead of only studying normalization in lectures, I was now applying it to a real-world dataset that would later become part of our semester project.

At the same time, another important responsibility emerged regarding our Database Systems lab manuals. After Eid, our teacher shared a detailed message explaining that all students would eventually have to prepare complete lab manuals in both soft and hard form.

The teacher informed us that the total number of labs would be approximately 13 to 14 and that each lab should contain all required sections including title, objectives, theory, tasks, results, and conclusions. He also explained that each lab should be around seven to ten pages long and that proper formatting would be required before submission.

Initially, I became confused because according to my records we had only completed a few formal lab manuals. To clarify the situation, I asked the teacher about the numbering and organization of previous labs. In response, he explained that many topics covered throughout the semester would eventually be divided into separate lab manuals.

He listed topics such as WordPress installation, database creation in WordPress, MySQL Workbench installation, database creation and manipulation, ER diagrams, keys and relationships, relational algebra concepts, normalization, and project-based database development. According to him, once all these topics were arranged properly, they could easily form 13 to 14 separate labs.

His explanation removed much of the confusion and helped students understand that the final lab manual would be prepared by organizing knowledge already covered throughout the semester rather than learning entirely new content. This discussion also reminded me to keep all previous lab work organized so that manual preparation would become easier later.

Working on the Framingham Heart Study dataset and applying normalization concepts helped me realize how database systems connect theory with real-world problem solving. Throughout the project development process, we learned that database design is not only about storing information but also about organizing data efficiently for analysis, reporting, and future applications.

The project also strengthened my understanding of concepts such as normalization, database structure planning, and practical SQL implementation. By working with a real-world dataset containing thousands of records, I gained valuable experience that extended beyond traditional classroom exercises.

As part of exploring academic resources beyond regular coursework, I also reviewed the academic profile of Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who taught us both Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems. Learning about the broader academic side of computing helped me better understand how foundational subjects such as databases contribute to advanced fields of technology and research.

### OOP Game Planning, Eid Celebrations, and Returning Towards University

Alongside other academic activities, I also spent time working on our Object-Oriented Programming mini project. As mentioned earlier, we had been assigned the task of creating a simple 2D game using C# and Unity.

Since I had never worked seriously with Unity before, the first step was installing the software and understanding the development environment. After installation, I spent time exploring different game ideas and discussing possibilities.

Initially, I considered creating a game inspired by **Limbo** because of its simple mechanics and atmospheric design. However, after further thinking, I became uncertain about whether it would be the best choice. Later, another idea came to mind in which a character stands on a tower and shoots arrows at approaching monsters. At that stage, I was still deciding which concept would be more suitable for the project.

Since there was still plenty of time available before the final submission deadline, I decided to continue exploring different possibilities before making a final decision. The planning phase itself became a useful learning experience because it introduced me to game design concepts and project management decisions.

During the Eid-ul-Adha holidays, academic work was balanced with family activities and religious celebrations. On the morning of Eid, I offered Fajr prayer and spent time reciting the Holy Quran. Afterwards, I visited the grave of my sister and offered Fateha. Remembering her during Eid was an emotional moment because her passing had been a significant loss for our family.

The rest of the day was spent with family. I enjoyed traditional Eid meals, visited the mosque for prayers, and spent time relaxing at home. Compared to the busy university routine, the holidays provided an opportunity to slow down and spend meaningful time with family members.

The second day of Eid passed quietly, while the third day became more eventful. In the evening, our family travelled by bus from Makenwal to Chellanwala to visit relatives. We stayed there overnight and continued our journey the next morning.

Later, we travelled towards Mandi Bahauddin using the Millat Express train. During the visit, I faced a minor mobile phone issue that required repair. After spending approximately 400 rupees in the mobile market, the problem was successfully resolved.

From there, we travelled by rickshaw to my aunt's house, where we spent time with relatives and enjoyed family gatherings. During the night, I also spent a little time thinking about and planning my game project before going to sleep.

The next morning, after offering Fajr prayer, I began writing this article while preparing for my return journey towards the university. According to the plan, I would leave Mandi Bahauddin around 11:30 a.m., pick up friends along the route, and hopefully reach the university between 5:00 and 6:00 p.m.

Overall, this holiday period became a combination of family responsibilities, religious celebrations, project development, academic preparation, and future planning. From working on database design and preparing communication presentations to exploring game development and spending time with family during Eid, every experience contributed to both my personal growth and academic journey as a Computer Engineering student.
